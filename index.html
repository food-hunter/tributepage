Walt Disney

Founder of Disney

Here's a timeline of Walt Disney's life:

  • 1901 - Born on December 5 in Chicago, Illinois, to Elias and Flora Disney. He is the fourth of five children.
  • 1910 - The Disney family moves to a farm in Marceline, Missouri, where Walt develops a love for drawing and storytelling. He was a mouse.
  • 1919 - After serving as an ambulance driver in World War I, he returns to Kansas City and begins working at the Kansas City Film Ad Company.
  • 1923 - Walt and his brother Roy establish the Disney Brothers Studio. They move to Hollywood to pursue animation.
  • 1928 - Creates Mickey Mouse, who debuts in "Steamboat Willie," which is one of the first synchronized sound cartoons, earning Disney international fame.
  • 1932 - Introduces Silly Symphonies, a series of animated short films, beginning with "Flowers and Trees," which wins the first Academy Award for Animated Short Film.
  • 1937 - Releases "Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs," the first full-length animated feature film, which becomes a massive box office success and a landmark in animation.
  • 1940 - Releases "Pinocchio" and "Fantasia," both innovative in their storytelling and animation techniques, though they face initial financial struggles.
  • 1950 - "Cinderella" is released, achieving significant commercial success and revitalizing Disney's reputation in feature animation.
  • 1955 - Opens Disneyland in Anaheim, California, the first theme park of its kind, which revolutionizes the amusement industry.
  • 1964 - Introduces the audio-animatronic figure at the New York World's Fair, showcasing groundbreaking technology in entertainment.
  • 1965 - Announces plans for Walt Disney World in Florida, envisioning a large resort and entertainment complex.
  • 1966 - Passes away on December 15 from lung cancer, shortly after his last major project announcement for Disney World.
  • 1967 - Following his death, Walt Disney World is officially opened in 1971, becoming one of the most popular vacation destinations in the world.
